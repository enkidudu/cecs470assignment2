Kameron White's education

Welcome! This site gives a historical profile of the post-secondary education of Kameron White

Undergraduate studies

I began my undergraduate studies in Fall '95 at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.

Taking 18 hours during the first semester of my freshman year, while running for student government, pledging a fraternity, and partying almost all of the time, was probably an ill-conceived execution of my original plan on going to medical school.

In the end, I managed to graduate in four years without having to take summer school and I had a really great time along the way! I earned a degree from the School of Journalism and Mass Communication, where I concentrated in advertising and integrated marketing. This degree has been valuable in helping to direct my web clients towards reaching their online marketing goals.

Career Transition

After graduating and moving to Los Angeles, I worked as an audio engineer for two years at Wild Woods doing post-production sound engineering for reality TV. Then, eight more years of sound engineering at Audio Mechanics, where I mastered soundtracks of many great classic films.

I ultimately discovered that digital sound engineering was limited by the tools that were included in the software applications provided to the sound engineer by a software engineer. That's when I decided that where I needed to be in the future wasn't sound engineering, but rather software engineering!

Since my undergraduate major is in the area of journalism, rather than engineering, I attended two years at Glendale Community College to earning prerequisite credits needed for enrollment into a graduate school for computer science.

While at GCC, I studied calculus and the following subjects in computer science:

  • Java
  • C++
  • Python
  • Algorithms
  • Data structures
  • Computer architecture
  • Assembly language
  • Networking

Graduate studies

When applying to graduate schools, I decided that the the Computer Engineering and Computer Science Department, at the California State University of Long Beach would be the best choice.

I began taking classes in Fall '11 and will become a candidate for graduation in 2014, upon completing five remaining courses.

While in school at Long Beach, I've had classes in the following subjects:

  • Software engineering
  • Net-centric computing
  • Database fundamentals
  • Design of dynamic websites
  • Object-oriented analysis & design
  • Advanced analysis of algorithms
  • Advanced operating systems
  • Advanced computer networking

Before graduating, I would like the opportunity to work on projects related to:

  • Ruby
  • C#.Net
  • Search engine technology
  • Mobile application development
  • JavaEE enterprise development
